Story 1: "My First Team" diversity as challenge and strength

"Five women from different countries - people who hadn't found their place in other teams"

üöß The Challenge

  • Language barriers: Communication was hindered by switching between English for work and Spanish as the vehicular language, each with its own cultural nuances.
  • Cultural differences: Diverse backgrounds resulted in different communication styles and expectations, often leading to misunderstandings or conflicts. For example, "Ahorita vengo" means different things to Latin Americans and Europeans.
  • Framework misalignment: The company operated under Agile, but my team was still using a waterfall approach, causing process friction.
  • Lack of standardized procedures: The absence of unified templates, mind maps or guides.
  • Team integration issues: members that hadn‚Äôt found their place in other teams, making it challenging to build trust and cohesion.
  • Different learning styles: individual needs that required extra flexibility and effort.
  • Continuous learning: professional growth was challenging due to varying levels of engagement with courses and learning platforms.
  • Empathetic leadership: creation of inclusive environment valuing diversity and each person‚Äôs unique role.

Story 2: "Leading Through Pandemic Uncertainty" adaptation and resilience

üöß The Challenge

  • Generational gap: 7 young professionals, different generation than mine
  • Gender gap: I was the only woman in a team, a challenging fact to build rapport and ensure my perspective was heard and valued
  • Abrupt remote transition: 100% remote work during lockdown
  • Connection challenge: "How do you get known without being known?"
  • Impostor syndrome: Learning technical topics while connecting emotionally
  • Team instability: Member departures and remote onboarding
  • Meeting overload: Productivity affected by excessive virtual meetings
  • Communication tools and meeting rules: We had to adapt to new tools for written and spoken communication. The team agreed on meeting rules to ensure everyone could participate and have their voice heard during virtual meetings.
"If I speak their language, I can understand them better. If I contribute ideas using their language, I'll be heard more attentively."

Story 3: Innovation and Leadership in AI era

üöß The Challenge

  • Exponential growth: From 7 to 35+ members, 8 nationalities, Spain & North Africa. Spanish is main language, English in some teams.
  • Diverse backgrounds: Mix of profiles as seniors with broad experience, mediors and juniors in their first job.
  • Information flow: Important messages may not reach everyone, causing errors in our processes, delivery delays and misalignment.
  • Knowledge silos: Lack of collaboration between QA leads and analysts lead to isolated work and poor knowledge sharing across the team.
  • Belonging & visibility: Individual contributions can go unnoticed, affecting motivation and turnover.
  • Technical evolution: Fast AI and tech changes make it hard to keep skills up to date.
  • Service efficiency: Too many simultaneous activities can lead to overload, making it easy to miss opportunities for improvement.
